Salutogenics and Social/Mental/Physical Honing:

  1. “Health is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease and infirmity. Health promotion, therefore, should proactively support, nurture, reinforce, and develop environments to increase engagement in activities that enhance physical, mental, and social capacity… Traditional measures use a ‘loss frame’ by measuring risks and problems. A ‘gain frame’ associates good habits with beneficial outcomes… Traditional efforts have created defensive health promotion efforts to eliminate risk factors rather than proactively going on offense to enhance capacity-building, sustainable lifestyles.” – Going on Offense to Enable Health Promotion Gains, American Journal of Health Promotion, September 2017
  2. (Note: This one is not a research study itself, but a reliable and comprehensive article supported by and referencing several research studies) – Personal and planetary health: We need a salutogenic approach!
  3. “Many studies have documented the benefits of minimizing negative health. The next evolutionary step should be toward optimization. Optimization refers to the most favorable relative conditions that facilitate measurable growth and success… such as high levels of performance. Measurement of salutogenic outcomes will require the assessment of progress toward reaching human potential.” – Salutogenesis 30 Years Later: Where Do We Go From Here?, International Electronic Journal of Health Education, 2010.
  4. “This paper explores intersections between health creation, leisure and nature emerging from research with Millennial university students in Australia… Most enter university believing that ‘health’ is a bio-medical (illness and disease focused) concept… …What emerges are insights into the manner in which leisure, nature and wellbeing intersect for these Millennials and… …can assist future health professionals to understand ‘health’ as not only bio-medical.” – Millennials Building Health (Salutogenesis) In Leisure and Nature, Annals of Leisure Research, April 2018
  5. (Note: This one is not an actual study, but an inspiring article about being “pro” what you desire, rather than “anti” what you fear) – Pro-Peace Not Anti-War, ReclaimingZen.
  6. “…differences [in emotional intelligence] have been shown to have a significant impact on important life outcomes (e.g., mental and physical health, work performance and social relationships). This study investigated, using a controlled experimental design, whether it is possible to increase EI… …Results showed a significant increase in emotion identification and emotion management abilities… Follow-up measures after 6 months revealed that these changes were persistent. These findings suggest that EI can be improved and open new treatment avenues.” – Increasing Emotional Intelligence: (How) Is It Possible?, Personality and Individual Differences Journal, 2009.

Negative Impacts of Technology on Youth Development:

  1. “Substantial increases in mental health difficulties, BMI and poor sleep-related behaviours highlight an increasing public health challenge.” – Changes in millennial adolescent mental health and health-related behaviours over 10 years: a population cohort comparison study, International Journal of Epidemiology, Volume 48, Issue 5, October 2019
  2. “Nearly a third of millennials have a behavioral health condition, and rates are rising by double digits. Millennials with a behavioral health condition are at twice the risk of having a chronic physical condition.” – Millenial Health: Trends In Behavioural Conditions, October 15th, 2020
  3. “Millennials are seeing their health decline faster than the previous generation as they age. This extends to both physical health conditions, such as hypertension and high cholesterol, and behavioral health conditions, such as major depression and hyperactivity. These accelerated declines will result in greater demand for treatment and higher healthcare costs… 33% higher than Gen-Xers experienced at a comparable age. Poorer health among millennials will… …manifest itself through higher unemployment and slower income growth.” – The Economic Consequences of Millennial Health, Health of America Report, Nov. 6, 2019.
  4. “…victims [of cyber bullying] were at increased risk for displaying psychotic experiences at age 18 and having suicidal ideation, attempts and completed suicides. Victims were also reported to have poor general health, including more bodily pain, headaches and slower recovery from illnesses. Moreover, victimised children were found to have lower educational qualifications, be worse at financial management and to earn less than their peers even at age 50. Victims were also reported to have more trouble making or keeping friends and to be less likely to live with a partner and have social support.” – Long-term effects of bullying, Archives of Disease in Childhood, 2015

Our Pedagogy (Teaching Methodology):

  1. “…a wide array of service-learning projects can positively impact students in each of these important areas [confidence, skills, performance]…” – Enhancing public speaking confidence, skills, and performance: An experiment of service-learning, The International Journal of Management Education.
  2. “…writing on physical paper can lead to more brain activity when remembering the information an hour later. Researchers say that the unique, complex, spatial and tactile information associated with writing by hand on physical paper is likely what leads to improved memory.” – Stronger Brain Activity After Writing On Paper Than On Tablet Or Smartphone, University of Tokyo Press, March 19, 2021
  3. “The significant superiority in both accuracy and activations for the Note group suggested that the use of a paper notebook promoted the acquisition of rich encoding information and/or spatial information of real papers and that this information could be utilized as effective retrieval clues, leading to higher activations in these specific regions.” – Paper Notebooks vs. Mobile Devices: Brain Activation Differences During Memory Retrieval, Frontiers in Behavioral Neuroscience, March 2021
  4. “The researchers suggest that the CBD [a type of journaling practice] is an effective tool for promoting positive feelings and supporting students’ basic psychological needs which are necessary to thrive and maintain motivation for sustained language learning [and presumably this translates to other types of learning].” – Promoting Positive Feelings and Motivation for Language Learning, Innovation in Language Learning and Teaching.
