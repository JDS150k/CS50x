Teaching Method

Smaph pedagogy is designed by educational experts to achieve three things.

  • Maximal attention from mentees - so we can deliver the challenging lessons
  • Maximal commitment from mentees - so they can do the challenging exercises
  • Maximal retention from mentees - so they can sustain the new changes over time

How do we design our teaching methods to achieve this? We use as much intensity, reality & novelty as possible.

  • Intense experiences are far more stimulating than moderate ones, rousing maximal attention
  • Real experiences are far more motivating than simulated ones, rousing maximal commitment
  • Novel experiences are far more memorable than orthodox ones, rousing maximal retention

Here are some of the techniques we use to ensure bootcamps are intense, real, and novel:

Filtering - ensures the right people are chosen for the courses).

Material Incentives - Prizes are given for top performers in the games and challenges.

Holistic discipline - the whole group will do press-ups if one mentee breaks the rules.

Blending - even purely mental topics, for example, are taught via a blend of physical/mental/social activities, to bring the potential of all three systems to the learning process.

Group and Solo Projects - to nurture both interdependence and independence.

Competitive and Cooperative Games - to nurture both egoism and collectivism.

Practical > Didactic - using projects, drills & exercises over lectures, presentations & worksheets.

Standing Up, Not Sitting Down - to keep the body and mind alert and active at all times.

No Screens - to bring the students into reality, and hold them there, without distractions.

On Location - making the day more unique & memorable by learning on location.

Team Lunch - a hearty and wholesome feast to refuel body, heart and mind together.

Real Life Situations - create challenges involving real people with real consequences.

Training Kits - take home kits to prime & instigate use of new skills in day-to-day life.

Prolonged Contact - sustains new habits after bootcamps for permanent change.

Maximal Intensity - going to extremes to make lessons more exciting and memorable.

Powerful Ratio - with 3-6 mentees per mentor, mentees get huge amounts of attention.

Parental Involvement - working with parents via consultations to modify each bootcamp according to each mentee’s personal needs.

Reporting - a detailed four-page report, with psychometric analysis, performance assessments, and expert commentaries on the mentees recent improvement, current state, and future potentials.

Bookend Module Structure - 1 weekend intensive bootcamp to teach the new skills/concepts, followed by 5 days of online mentoring to integrate the skills/concepts into real life, before another weekend intensive bootcamp to consolidate and lock-in the changes.

Locking-In - closing each module with a 1:1 mentorial meeting during which the mentee will be enthused to lock-in to some commitments to continued application of the new skills/concepts.

Follow-up - Meeting mentees again 3 months after the module for another 1:1 mentorial, to reflect on how well they’ve integrated the new skills/concepts into their life.
